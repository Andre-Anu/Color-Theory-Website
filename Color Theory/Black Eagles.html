Nestled within the verdant halls of Garreg Mach Monastery, the Golden Deer house was a lively and eclectic group of students, each harboring dreams as diverse as the colors of autumn leaves. Led by the spirited and ever-curious Claude, the Golden Deer were a force to be reckoned with, not only on the battlefield but also in the corridors of the academy where laughter and mischief echoed.

At the helm of this merry band was Claude, a charming and quick-witted archer with a penchant for unraveling mysteries. His leadership style was unconventional, blending strategic acumen with a laid-back demeanor that endeared him to his peers. The Golden Deer were not merely a house; they were a family bound by a shared love for adventure and a thirst for knowledge.

Lorenz, the suave and sophisticated noble, added a touch of refinement to the group. His aspirations for a more just and equitable Fódlan often clashed with his aristocratic background, creating a complex character who constantly strived for balance.

Then there was Raphael, a gentle giant with a heart of gold and muscles that could rival any seasoned warrior. His love for food and unwavering loyalty made him a pillar of strength within the Golden Deer, proving that true courage came in many forms.
